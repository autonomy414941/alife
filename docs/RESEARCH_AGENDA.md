# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Expand Representational Capacity by replacing the fixed three-axis genome with an extensible trait architecture that supports add/remove loci mutations. Phase 1 (GenomeV2 infrastructure) is complete with full test coverage. The factorial confirmed mechanism-combination tuning is exhausted: composition-dependent conditions went extinct while composition-agnostic conditions remained deeply negative versus null. Structural expansion is now required to unblock cumulative innovation and the complexity ratchet.

## Why This Direction
The 2×2 factorial executed at canonical 4000-step horizon (commit 35341c1) confirmed null results across all four mechanism combinations: composition-dependent conditions (both dominant+dependent and non-transitive+dependent) went extinct (finalPopulation=0), while composition-agnostic conditions survived but remained deeply negative versus null (dominant agnostic: -35 active clades, non-transitive agnostic: -50.5). The 30/30 composition gate proved lethal because agents cannot maintain balanced pools under composition-agnostic metabolism. This decisively confirms that the three-axis genome ceiling structurally caps partitioning tradeoffs regardless of constraint tuning. The structural expansion decision memo (commit 459a65c) analyzed 14 Critic-flagged ceiling items and recommends Representational Capacity as the highest-leverage expansion: replacing the fixed genome schema with an extensible trait map unblocks cumulative innovation, directly addresses Aevol complexity-ratchet and ASAL open-endedness findings (unbounded complexity increase requires genomes that can grow structurally), and serves as the foundation for future behavioral, interaction, and environmental expansions. Implementation plan spans four phases: (1) extensible genome with add/remove mutation, (2) promote habitat/trophic/defense to first-class evolvable loci, (3) complexity-ratchet metrics tracking genome size and phenotype diversity, (4) validation against canonical baseline. Expected falsification signal: if extensible genome shows same nullity as fixed genome, then representational capacity alone is insufficient and combination with behavioral or environmental expansion is needed.

## Structural Constraints
The genome is fixed at three mutable axes (`metabolism`, `harvest`, `aggression`) plus optional `harvestEfficiency2`, with all other ecological roles (habitat preference, trophic level, defense) derived from these axes. This compile-time schema structurally prevents cumulative innovation: agents cannot evolve new sensors, metabolic pathways, life stages, or interaction modes because those capabilities are not representable in the `Genome` type. The factorial demonstrated this ceiling: composition-dependent reproduction (30% primary + 30% secondary) is lethal under composition-agnostic metabolism, and no parameter tuning can fix the mismatch because metabolism cannot become composition-aware within the current architecture. Expanding what agents can evolve requires replacing the fixed genome with an extensible trait system that supports adding/removing loci through mutation.
