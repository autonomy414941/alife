# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Execute the composition-dependent physiology mechanism-combination factorial, analyze whether the implemented partitioning constraints produce measurable coexistence gains, and if results remain null across all mechanism combinations, shift toward structural expansion targeting the complexity ratchet rather than continuing parameter-surface mechanism tuning within the current three-axis genome ceiling.

## Why This Direction
The last session completed all four targeted infrastructure bets: composition-dependent reproduction landed (requiring minimum 30% primary and 30% secondary energy fractions), substrate observability metrics are wired, non-transitive operator validation at canonical horizon confirmed zero delta versus dominant (both converge to single active clade), and the 2×2 factorial study code exists but has not yet run. The factorial is now the critical decision point: if composition-dependent costs combined with non-transitive topology still produce zero interaction effect and zero absolute gains versus null, then the evidence will strongly support that the current expressiveness ceiling (three-axis genome, composition-agnostic metabolism, fungible total-energy physiology despite dual pools) structurally caps partitioning tradeoffs regardless of constraint tuning. Recent artificial life literature on open-endedness (ASAL 2025, complexity ratchet findings from Aevol platform) emphasizes that continual generation of novelty and unbounded complexity increase require systems that can accumulate structural innovations, not just optimize within a fixed mechanism set. The backlog contains 14 structural-ceiling items flagged by the Critic across six dimensions (Representational Capacity, Interaction Richness, Environmental Complexity, Evolutionary Mechanisms, Behavioral Control, Inheritance Architecture), while the last 10 commits show mechanism implementation (5/10) and validation (3/10) dominating exploration, with zero commits on structural expansion. If the factorial shows null results, the Planner should pivot from mechanism-combination tuning toward one high-leverage structural expansion that unblocks the complexity ratchet.

## Structural Constraints
The `canReproduce()` gate now checks `reproductionMinPrimaryFraction` and `reproductionMinSecondaryFraction` thresholds (lines 111-130 in `src/simulation-reproduction.ts`), but `spendAgentEnergy()` still debits both pools proportionally regardless of composition, and all other physiology (movement cost, metabolism, encounter transfers, disturbance impacts) keys off total energy alone. This creates a reproductive filter on pool balance but does not impose differential survival or metabolic efficiency based on resource specialization, limiting how much partitioning tradeoffs can express. The non-transitive encounter operator produces identical convergence outcomes to dominant operator because energy transfers depend only on total energy and aggression, not on internal composition or resource-specialization match between predator and prey. The genome remains three mutable axes (`metabolism`, `harvest`, `aggression`) with `harvestEfficiency2` as an optional fourth scalar, while all other ecological roles (habitat preference, trophic level, defense) are derived summaries computed from these three axes—no amount of mechanism tuning can produce evolvable sensors, life stages, mate choice, or alternative metabolic pathways because those capabilities are not representable in the current `Genome` schema. The factorial will test whether reproductive gating alone suffices for coexistence gains, but if not, the path forward requires expanding what agents can evolve, not retuning how the current three axes interact.
