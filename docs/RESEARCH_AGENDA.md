# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Over the next month, shift from mechanism-seam extraction toward mechanism-combination experiments. The pairwise encounter operator validation (`886e509`) produced zero-delta outcomes versus dominant-only despite structural differences, confirming the Critic's diagnosis: encounter topology alone cannot break structural ceilings without complementary substrate heterogeneity. The next sessions should test whether resource-layer expansion plus encounter-topology variation create synergistic coexistence gains that neither mechanism produces alone.

## Why This Direction
The strongest anti-evidence remains absolute: no configuration beats its species-conditioned relabel null in absolute active clades. The encounter operator comparative study (`886e509`) confirmed that pairwise versus dominant topology produces identical final outcomes (both zero delta versus null at all tested thresholds). The Critic's diagnosis is structural: under one fungible resource pool, trophic specialization collapses to a single axis, and niche differentiation is limited to habitat preference plus aggression gradients. Recent literature (Ecology Letters 2025, Theoretical Population Biology 2025) emphasizes emergent coexistence from mechanism combinations extending persistence more than tenfold versus singular mechanisms. The memory-scaling study (`24820b3`) confirmed linear scaling up to 5000 steps (~30MB heap), ruling out history-memory as an immediate blocker but revealing that the current study surface runs at 100-step horizons while the canonical artifacts run at 4000 steps. The gap is now compositional: the planner built the encounter seam and validated it structurally, but then tested the new operator in isolation on a 100-step micro-horizon instead of combining it with resource heterogeneity or running at the canonical 4000-step horizon where slow coexistence regimes could emerge.

## Structural Constraints
`src/simulation.ts` (1926 lines) still concentrates most runtime responsibilities. Encounter resolution has been extracted to `src/encounter.ts` with a pluggable `EncounterOperator` interface plus two implementations (dominant and pairwise), creating the first tested mechanism seam. However, the simulator remains locked to one fungible resource/energy currency, three mutable genome axes, greedy one-step local choice, and single-parent clonal inheritance. The pairwise operator validation revealed that interaction topology diversity alone produces no differentiation when all agents compete for the same resource pool in the same way. Resource harvesting, metabolism, predation, and reproduction all debit/credit a single shared `energy` scalar, while cells contain a single `resource` scalar. This topology prevents resource partitioning, specialist-generalist tradeoffs, or multi-nutrient co-limitation.
