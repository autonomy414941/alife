# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Over the next month, the project should determine whether the April 2 mechanism prototypes can produce adaptive policy-bearing cohorts once they are wired into the live simulation and evaluated under stricter evidence standards. The priority is to activate action selection, context-dependent phenotype realization, and richer observations inside the main turn loop, then test whether the observed multi-horizon survival gains survive better matching and replay-grounded validation.

## Why This Direction
April 2 changed the evidence landscape. The multi-horizon validation artifact (`docs/multi_horizon_policy_credit_assignment_2026-04-02.json`) found 104 positive horizon effects, with the strongest signal at `strict|guarded|primary` and +20-tick survival advantage of +0.0661, so delayed policy payoffs are plausible. At the same time, repository inspection shows that `src/action-selection.ts` is still unused by the live simulation and context-aware `realizePhenotype(..., context)` is only exercised in tests and the spike script, which means the main system still runs fixed-repertoire, mostly direct-encoded agents despite the new prototypes.

The strongest current risk is therefore not that the mechanism search is empty, but that the project treats prototype-only code or stale artifacts as evidence about the live system. At least one checked-in artifact (`docs/signature_specific_reproduction_gate_ablation_2026-04-02.json`) still reflects the older reanalysis path while the source module now implements replay-branch ablation, so the next month should prioritize live activation and evidence freshness before drawing structural conclusions.

Recent literature still supports prioritizing expressiveness and feedback fidelity over adding more coarse summary metrics. Latorre et al., "Simulating macroevolutionary trends and open-ended evolution with a novel mechanistic multi-level approach" (PLOS ONE 20(1), 2025, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0335033) emphasize generative genotype-to-phenotype mapping plus lower-level mechanisms, while the 2024 plasticity study "Facilitation of Evolution by Plasticity Scales with Phenotypic Complexity" (Animals 14(19):2804, https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11476054/) shows that environmentally responsive phenotype construction can accelerate the acquisition of complex adaptive systems.

## Structural Constraints
- The live turn loop still hard-codes movement, harvest, and reproduction in a fixed order; `action_priority_*` and `action_threshold_*` loci exist but do not yet drive `processAgentTurn`
- Context-dependent phenotype realization currently modulates only metabolic efficiency in test and spike code; the live simulation still does not pass local ecological context into phenotype realization for core decisions
- Policy validation still relies on coarse fertility/crowding/age/disturbance bins, so the April 2 multi-horizon positives may be matching artifacts rather than genuine context-conditional advantages
- Adult movement and offspring settlement still optimize ground-truth ecology scores, not a limited observation layer, so the architecture cannot test uncertainty-sensitive behavior or perception-driven policy state
- Artifact generation is not tightly coupled to source evolution, so stale or methodology-drifted evidence can survive in `docs/` and mislead planning unless explicitly refreshed
