The current substrate preserves pool identity but lacks composition-dependent constraints—the blocker preventing partitioning tradeoffs from expressing in coexistence outcomes.

## Structural Constraints
`src/simulation.ts` concentrates runtime responsibilities with three mechanism seams: (1) encounter resolution (`src/encounter.ts` with `EncounterOperator` interface, three implementations: dominant, pairwise, non-transitive), (2) dual-resource substrate (`src/resource-harvest.ts` with `resources2` grid and `harvestEfficiency2` genome axis), (3) internal energy pools (`src/agent-energy.ts` preserving `energyPrimary` and `energySecondary` separately through metabolism and reproduction). Pairwise versus dominant operators produce zero differentiation at canonical 4000-step horizon. The critical structural ceiling is that metabolism (`spendAgentEnergy`) and reproduction scale both pools proportionally regardless of composition—specialists and generalists with matched total energy face identical viability constraints because costs do not depend on pool balance or genome-defined target ratios. Without composition-dependent physiology, the dual-resource substrate cannot express partitioning tradeoffs: agents that harvest different resource mixes remain internally distinguishable but functionally equivalent in downstream survival and reproduction.
