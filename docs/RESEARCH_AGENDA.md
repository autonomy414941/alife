# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Over the next month, the project should determine whether perception-driven spatial choice under partial observability can produce context-conditional adaptive advantages that perfect-information omniscient agents cannot achieve. The priority is to replace direct ground-truth ecology access with limited sensed observations for movement and settlement decisions, make perception quality evolvable, and test whether perception-driven behavior outperforms omniscient baseline under matched validation.

## Why This Direction
April 3 resolved the live-activation gap: context-dependent phenotype realization now drives harvest decisions in the main loop, local observation summaries exist on every agent, and action-selection loci are quarantined from distance calculations until they become behaviorally active. The multi-horizon April 2 signal (+0.0661 survival at +20 ticks) remains unverified under April 3's richer matching, but the infrastructure is now in place to test perception-driven mechanisms.

The strongest remaining structural gap is that movement and settlement still optimize ground-truth ecology scores (direct reads of `fertility`, `crowding`, `lineage occupancy`) rather than limited sensed observations. This prevents the architecture from testing whether partial observability creates different adaptive landscapes, whether perception quality should be evolvable, or whether robust policies can outperform omniscient-but-brittle strategies.

Recent RL literature confirms that partial observability can improve generalization and force active perception behaviors. Guided Policy Optimization under Partial Observability (arXiv:2505.15418, 2025) shows that constrained learners can match privileged-information performance, and Active Vision RL (NeurIPS 2023) demonstrates that sensory control policies emerge when agents must actively manage limited observations. These results suggest that replacing perfect-information spatial choice with perception-driven decision surfaces is a structural upgrade, not just a parameter variation.

## Structural Constraints
- Adult movement and offspring settlement still optimize ground-truth ecology scores (direct reads of `fertility`, `crowding`, `lineage occupancy`) instead of limited sensed observations, so the architecture cannot test perception-driven spatial choice or evolvable perception quality
- The observation map exists but is not yet consumed by movement or settlement logic, so sensory limitations cannot yet affect spatial choice outcomes
- Perception quality is not yet genetically encoded, so agents cannot evolve different sensory strategies or trade off perception fidelity against other traits
- Action-selection loci remain quarantined and unused by the live turn loop, so discrete action choice and fallback sequences are still structurally out of reach
- The April 3 richer-context matching artifact has not been analyzed, so whether the April 2 multi-horizon survival gains survive stricter matching remains unknown
