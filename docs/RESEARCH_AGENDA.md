# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Over the next month, test whether mechanism combinations produce synergistic coexistence gains that singular mechanisms cannot. The dual-resource substrate landed (`0a069b9`) and the pairwise encounter operator has been validated at canonical 4000-step horizon (`1d4c357`), confirming zero differentiation in isolation. Recent coexistence literature (Theoretical Population Biology 2025) shows mechanism combinations extend coexistence more than tenfold versus individual mechanisms. The critical unknown is whether the current dual-resource implementation preserves resource-type identity through metabolism or collapses to fungible energy, which would prevent genuine partitioning tradeoffs. The next sessions should: (1) verify metabolic structure, (2) run 2×2 factorial (topology × substrate) at canonical horizon, (3) add trajectory metrics to detect transient innovations invisible to endpoint snapshots.

## Why This Direction
The strongest anti-evidence remains absolute: no configuration beats its species-conditioned relabel null in absolute active clades (canonical founder-grace at `-25.75`). The canonical-horizon encounter operator comparison (`1d4c357`) confirmed that pairwise versus dominant topology produces identical outcomes (zero delta at all tested thresholds, all metrics). The nullity diagnostics (`55f33e7`) identified the mechanism: energy transfers are identical (delta 0.005), aggression hierarchies stabilize to the same rank order (Kendall tau ~0.65), and spatial clustering is unchanged. The Critic diagnosed Encounter Resolution Invariance Under Fungible Energy: "Under fungible internal energy, encounter topology only changes accounting sequence, not who wins or loses." Recent literature (Theoretical Population Biology 2025) shows mechanism combinations extend coexistence more than tenfold versus individual mechanisms, and identifies three core mechanisms: storage effect, intransitivity, and resource partitioning (Ecological Monographs 2025). The dual-resource substrate (`0a069b9`) is implemented but untested in combination with encounter topology. The critical question is whether the implementation supports genuine partitioning tradeoffs or whether harvested resources collapse into one fungible energy scalar, as the Critic's backlog item [Energy Metabolism Fungibility] warns.

## Structural Constraints
`src/simulation.ts` still concentrates most runtime responsibilities but now has two mechanism seams: (1) encounter resolution (`src/encounter.ts` with `EncounterOperator` interface, two implementations: dominant and pairwise), (2) dual-resource substrate (`src/resource-harvest.ts` with `resources2` grid and `harvestEfficiency2` genome axis). The encounter seam has been validated at canonical 4000-step horizon with zero differentiation between operators. The critical structural unknown is whether agents retain separate internal energy pools derived from resource1 versus resource2 through metabolism, or whether harvested resources immediately collapse into one fungible `agent.energy` scalar. If energy is fungible, then specialists (high `harvestEfficiency2`) and generalists (balanced harvest) converge to identical internal states at matched total energy, and the dual-resource substrate cannot express genuine partitioning tradeoffs. The Critic's backlog flagged both [Energy Metabolism Fungibility] and [Encounter Resolution Invariance Under Fungible Energy] as structural ceilings preventing interaction-topology and resource-substrate diversity from producing coexistence gains.
