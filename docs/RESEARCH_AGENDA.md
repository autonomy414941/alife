# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Over the next month, the project should determine whether richer action selection, multi-horizon credit assignment, or context-dependent phenotype realization can rescue policy-bearing cohorts from their current matched-control detriment, or whether the fundamental ceiling is the direct-encoding architecture itself. The priority is to test bounded expansions along the action model, feedback horizon, and perception/memory axes before concluding that policy evolution is structurally unreachable.

## Why This Direction
April 1 materially refined the mechanism attribution. The operator ablation artifact (`docs/policy_operator_sign_ablation_panel_2026-04-01.json`) isolated reproduction gating as the most harmful operator (net diversification -1.15, effective richness -1.59, occupied niches -3.00), while reserve spending was near-neutral (net diversification 0.00, effective richness -0.04, occupied niches 0.00). The signature validation artifact (`docs/policy_signature_matched_control_validation_2026-04-01.json`) identified one signature (`open|strict|balanced`) with consistent positive harvest advantage (+0.0477 weighted), though reproduction and survival remained near-neutral or negative. The memory horizon artifact (`docs/memory_horizon_validation_2026-04-01.json`) confirmed that rolling-window and decay-weighted harvest memory diverges measurably from single-tick state, providing a substrate for temporal integration.

These results show that the aggregate March 31 failure is not uniform: harvest guidance shows small gains in specific signatures, reproduction gating is broadly harmful, and richer memory substrates exist. The strongest current risk is not missing one more coarse metric but prematurely concluding that policy evolution cannot work when the action model, credit horizon, and state representation remain constrained.

Recent literature supports prioritizing expressiveness and feedback fidelity over adding more summary metrics. Latorre et al., "Simulating macroevolutionary trends and open-ended evolution with a novel mechanistic multi-level approach" (PLOS ONE 20(1), 2025, https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0335033) show that multi-scale process-based frameworks can bridge microevolution and macroevolution by integrating genotype-to-phenotype mapping with biotic interactions and lower-level mechanisms. Bedau et al., "The MODES Toolbox: Measurements of Open-Ended Dynamics in Evolving Systems" (Artificial Life 25(1), 2019, https://doi.org/10.1162/artl_a_00280) argue that open-endedness measurement should target change potential, novelty potential, complexity potential, and ecological potential, not just coarse diversity counts.

## Structural Constraints
- Agents have a fixed behavioral repertoire: move or stay, harvest, reproduce if threshold met, with variation only through continuous trait modulation, not discrete action selection or sequencing
- Policy-fitness matching bins decision context into only fertility, crowding, age, and disturbance phase, while ignoring resource mix, habitat mismatch, local lineage composition, and exact policy thresholds, so behaviorally different exposures are treated as matched
- Policy-fitness scoring uses only same-tick harvest, survival, and reproduction, so delayed consequences of movement, reserve spending, or cautious reproduction are invisible
- Default initial agents still start without `genomeV2` unless studies explicitly override initialization, creating an infrastructure mismatch that silently disables policy evolution in vanilla runs
- All traits are still expressed as genotype scalars with no explicit context-dependent phenotype construction stage, limiting how much policy state can be conditioned on local context
