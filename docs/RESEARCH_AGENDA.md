# Research Agenda

## Current Direction
Turn policy loci from mostly threshold and allocation knobs into ecologically consequential strategies that can change survival, energy flow, and diversification in ways the simulator can explain. Over the next month, the focus is to couple policy traits directly to payoffs in the asymmetric two-resource world, adopt a defensible taxonomic-distance default, and add enough causal traceability that diversification claims are tied to mechanism instead of aggregate counts alone.

## Why This Direction
The previous monthly bottlenecks were materially reduced on March 28. A shared phenotype decoder now routes supported ecological and policy loci into live operators, the secondary resource layer has independent spatial and seasonal controls, and standard summaries now expose phenotype-diversity metrics in addition to raw species and clade counts. However, the bounded graded-policy neutrality panel on 2026-03-28 still found only weak ecological gains for the policy-enabled arm: effective richness increased by 11.2% and occupied niches by 13.3%, while speciation rate fell by 53.8%. That is stronger than pure neutrality, but it is not yet evidence that policy traits are driving cumulative diversification.

The repo evidence points to a narrower ceiling than before. Policy traits now affect gating and resource allocation, but they still rarely modify ecological payoffs directly; harvest efficiency, encounter outcomes, settlement scoring, and metabolic costs remain dominated by morphology-side parameters. The new phenotype-diversity metrics also summarize `NON_POLICY_TRAITS`, so policy-driven differentiation is only visible when policy changes feed through to non-policy ecological state. In parallel, the March 28 validation used the morphology-priority distance regime even though the March 27 calibration analysis rejected that weighting as a default. The system therefore still lacks a stable bridge between policy variation, ecological consequence, and taxonomic interpretation.

Recent external context reinforces that shift. Moreno, Rodriguez-Papa, and Dolson, "Ecology, Spatial Structure, and Selection Pressure Induce Strong Signatures in Phylogenetic Structure" (arXiv:2405.07245, revised 2024-11-21; related Artificial Life publication) shows that ecology and spatial structure can leave detectable phylogenetic signatures, but also argues that further methods are needed to distinguish drivers and normalize phylometric evidence. I infer from that result that this repo now needs higher-resolution causal and genealogical observability before treating metric movement as mechanism evidence. Faldor and Cully, "Toward Artificial Open-Ended Evolution within Lenia using Quality-Diversity" (ALIFE 2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.04235) likewise points toward richer expressive spaces becoming valuable only when they are tied to niches and sustained diversity pressure.

## Structural Constraints
- Policy traits mostly gate movement, harvest, reproduction, and spending choices, but they still do not directly modulate core ecological payoffs such as intake efficiency, encounter transfer, settlement success, or metabolic burden
- `phenotypeDiversity` is now available, but it is built from `NON_POLICY_TRAITS`, so policy-driven ecological separation can remain partly invisible unless policy variation changes morphology-side phenotype
- `genomeV2DistanceWeights` adopted default: `{ categories: { policyThreshold: 0.25, policyBounded: 0.5 } }` per March 27 calibration and March 29 follow-up validation, which found this regime produces outcomes identical to morphology-priority under the current post-decoder, asymmetric-ecology stack while avoiding the mixed-divergence inflation that rejected morphology-priority
- The simulator still lacks sampled per-event attribution and lightweight genealogy that would show which policy decisions, trait combinations, and lineages actually produced or suppressed diversification
- The study layer still cannot branch a live world into paired counterfactual runs, so mechanism comparisons remain exposed to path dependence even when seeds are matched
