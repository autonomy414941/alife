- [expand] [Environmental Complexity] The world is a static fertility map plus one fungible resource pool with exogenous disturbance; agents can deplete and recycle resources but cannot persistently construct, poison, shelter, or otherwise rewrite habitat. Intervention: add persistent per-cell state layers that agents deposit, erode, and sense over many ticks, then feed those layers back into movement, harvest, and survival.
- [expand] [Trait Decoder Bottleneck] `GenomeV2` can store arbitrary trait keys, but the live simulator still routes ecology through hard-coded reads of `metabolism`, `harvest`, `aggression`, `harvestEfficiency2`, and taxon-level habitat / trophic / defense caches. Under this architecture any newly added locus is selectively inert baggage until a human wires it into a specific operator, so schema expansion alone cannot yield new phenotypes or interactions. Intervention: replace per-operator hard-coded trait access with a trait-expression registry or phenotype decoder that maps declared loci into metabolism, harvest, encounter, movement, settlement, and taxonomic decisions. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.
- [expand] [Interaction Richness] The interaction alphabet is compile-time fixed to movement, harvest, same-cell energy theft, reproduction, and thresholded clade founding, so lineages cannot evolve new interaction types such as signaling, cooperation, public goods, or alternative predation / resource-conversion modes. Intervention: generalize encounters and foraging into evolvable action channels over multiple resource or field layers instead of a single hard-coded transfer equation.
- [expand] [Inheritance Architecture] Every descendant is created by single-parent clonal mutation, with speciation and cladogenesis as irreversible threshold side effects of that one lineage. Within this framework the system cannot express recombination, sexual selection, mating compatibility, or horizontal trait transfer, so innovation is limited to local mutation walks around one parent genome. Intervention: introduce multi-parent inheritance or exchange operators plus compatibility / partner-choice rules, and make parentage an explicit part of descendant creation.
- [expand] [Evolutionary Mechanisms] Speciation and cladogenesis are single-offspring threshold events in a wrapped torus with no barriers, demes, mate choice, or lineage-specific breeding structure, so the system cannot express allopatric isolation, reproductive barriers, or adaptive radiation driven by sustained separation. Intervention: add patch barriers or a patch graph plus lineage-local colonization / breeding pools, and require sustained ecological or spatial separation before promoting a new clade.
- [feat] [Descent Observability] `TaxonHistory` retains only per-taxon counts and timelines; the simulation discards parent-child edges, founder trait snapshots beyond a clade founder genome, and phenotype transitions at speciation / cladogenesis time. That makes cumulative novelty, convergence, evolutionary dead ends, and innovation reuse structurally unmeasurable no matter how the Planner reshapes current studies. Intervention: persist a lightweight genealogy / innovation graph with parent ids, founder traits, and phenotype deltas at taxon birth events, then derive novelty and reuse metrics from that graph.
- [validate] [Temporal Credit Assignment] The study surface mostly aggregates final snapshots, fixed-window turnover, and persistence-threshold deltas, so interventions that create repeated but short-lived innovations or long transient scaffolding before late collapse are nearly invisible to the current optimization loop. Under the present framework the system can optimize endpoint persistence proxies, but not ratcheting search over trajectory quality. Intervention: add first-class trajectory metrics such as area under active-clade diversity, innovation survival curves, regime-switch counts, and recolonization-time distributions alongside the existing final-state summaries.
- [refactor] [Trait Granularity] Habitat preference, trophic level, and defense level live in taxon-level scalar maps and are only updated on speciation or clade founding, while movement, harvest, and encounters consume those taxon summaries instead of individual phenotype. That makes within-species ecological polymorphism, frequency-dependent morph coexistence, and gradual niche divergence inside an extant species structurally impossible; ecological differentiation only becomes visible after a taxonomic split. Intervention: move ecological phenotype fields to heritable per-agent state updated every birth, and derive species / clade summaries from those distributions instead of using taxon-level lookup tables as the source of truth.
- [expand] [Dispersal Kinematics] Adults can only stay put or move one cardinal step per tick, and offspring can only settle in the parent cell or its four cardinal neighbors; `dispersalRadius` changes crowding calculations, not move or settlement distance. No amount of parameter tuning can produce rare long-range colonization, dispersal polymorphisms, migration fronts with variable jump lengths, or metapopulation rescue via occasional distant propagules. Intervention: separate sensing radius from movement / settlement kernels, then add configurable or evolvable step-length and offspring-dispersal distributions including rare long-distance events.
- [validate] [Taxonomic Proxy Leakage] The optimization surface still scores success mainly through species / clade counts, persistence windows, turnover, and relabel-null deltas, but taxon identities are minted by threshold crossings rather than by demonstrated ecological distinctness. That means the Planner can win on paper through ecologically near-identical taxonomic splits or by preserving labels without opening new niches, and the current summaries will often treat that as diversification. Intervention: add ecology-weighted diversity metrics that discount near-duplicate taxa, such as phenotype-distance-weighted richness, effective niche occupancy, and interaction-distinct clade counts, and make those co-equal with raw taxon counts in study decisions.
- [validate] [Matched-Null Fidelity] The relabel-null baseline preserves clade birth counts by tick, then randomly reassigns species into pseudo-clades without preserving founder habitat, local crowding, abundance class, or disturbance context. Under that proxy, a stack can look better than null while still failing to create genuinely distinct ecological opportunities. Intervention: add stricter null families that also match founder habitat bin, local occupancy / lineage crowding, abundance class, and disturbance phase at birth before treating delta-vs-null gains as evidence of progress.
- [feat] [Mechanistic Causality] `TaxonHistory` tracks births, deaths, and population counts, but the system does not retain cause-specific losses, harvest intake, predation transfers, movement costs, or disturbance impacts by species / clade. That makes it structurally impossible to tell whether a reported gain came from better founding, lower same-lineage crowding, reduced predation, or simple resource hoarding. Intervention: add per-taxon demographic and energy ledgers with cause tags, then surface founder-phase and maintenance-phase budgets in the study layer.
- [expand] [Finite Locus Catalog] `mutateGenomeV2()` only adds loci from a compile-time `candidateNewLoci` list, `EXTENDED_TRAITS` is human-enumerated, and the current adapter path still permits only `harvestEfficiency2` during live reproduction. That means the apparent extensible genome still cannot invent unforeseen trait kinds no matter how long evolution runs; it can only toggle a small human-approved menu. Intervention: replace fixed candidate-name lists with a trait-module factory or parameterized locus generator that can mint new loci families and persist their metadata in heritable state. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [refactor] [Shared Scalar Geometry Assumption] `GenomeV2` currently models every locus as a bounded scalar with the same mutation operator, default fallback, and unweighted L1 distance, even though existing physiology already uses heterogeneous trait scales and would need categorical, thresholded, or structured state for richer mechanisms. Within that geometry, added complexity is limited to "more numbers in a map," while speciation and cladogenesis remain sensitive to arbitrary scaling choices rather than semantic novelty. Intervention: introduce per-locus metadata for domain, bounds or transforms, mutation kernel, and distance weight, and allow typed loci or small modules instead of only `string -> number` scalars. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.
- [validate] [Legacy Metric Surface] `StepSummary`, CSV exports, and aggregate experiment outputs still hard-code mean and selection-differential reporting to `metabolism`, `harvest`, and `aggression`, while strategy analytics remain restricted to habitat / trophic / defense summaries. If new loci begin evolving, the main feedback loop cannot see whether they spread, matter, or drive novelty, so Planner optimization remains locked to the legacy triad even after representational expansion. Intervention: add generic trait-distribution, loci-count, add/remove event, and trait-expression metrics to run summaries and study exports, then make them co-equal with taxon-count outcomes in experiment decisions. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [expand] [One-Dimensional Habitat Axis] `effectiveBiomeFertilityAt()` exposes habitat as a single scalar and `habitatMatchEfficiency()` compares lineages against that one value, while `buildBiomeFertility()` generates only one underlying abiotic field. Even if GenomeV2 adds richer habitat loci, the current world can only reward specialization along one productivity gradient, so orthogonal abiotic tradeoffs such as wet vs dry, warm vs cold, exposed vs sheltered, or substrate-type mosaics are structurally impossible. Intervention: replace the single fertility field with a vector of abiotic layers and let habitat phenotype encode multi-axis tolerance or preference over that field bundle. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Diet-Choice Compression] `resolveDualResourceHarvest()` and `combinedResourceAvailability()` force each agent to consume and value the two resource layers in a fixed ratio derived from `genome.harvest` and `harvestEfficiency2`. Under that architecture agents cannot conditionally switch diets based on local scarcity, internal pool deficits, disturbance phase, or competitor presence; selective foraging is reduced to a static weighted average baked into morphology. Intervention: separate substrate preference / effort allocation from processing efficiency, add a harvest-choice policy that can reallocate effort across resource layers from observations and internal state, and make movement / settlement score candidate cells against those chosen allocations instead of one hard-coded mix. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.
- [validate] [Energy-As-Fitness Proxy] `selectionDifferential()` weights trait success by current stored energy, `StepSummary` centers mean-energy and legacy-trait snapshots, and `runExperiment()` aggregates only final summaries plus turnover analytics. That feedback loop cannot distinguish traits that sacrifice short-term energy for higher descendant output, delayed establishment, or downstream founding from traits that merely hoard energy without propagating, so optimization can drift toward state proxies rather than realized evolutionary success. Intervention: add lagged descendant-contribution and reproductive-value metrics by trait, lineage, and taxon, and report both immediate state proxies and delayed descendant outcomes in experiment aggregates. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [expand] [Life-History Compression] `Agent` state contains age, position, energy pools, taxonomy, and genome, but age only increments toward a hard `maxAge` kill switch while the same movement, harvest, encounter, and reproduction operators apply from birth to death. Within that architecture the system cannot express juvenile vs adult niches, maturation delay, reproductive senescence, dormant stages, or condition-dependent life-history switching; every lineage is forced into one immortal-until-cutoff adult template. Intervention: add explicit life-stage or size state with stage-specific metabolism, movement, harvest, encounter, and reproduction kernels, and let transitions depend on age, condition, and heritable parameters. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.
- [expand] [Single-Offspring Reproduction Kernel] `runReproductionPhase()` gives each eligible parent at most one reproduction attempt per tick, and `reproduceAgent()` always emits exactly one child funded by the fixed `offspringEnergyFraction`. No amount of planner tuning can produce clutch-size tradeoffs, brood splitting, semelparity vs iteroparity, many-small vs few-large offspring strategies, or reproductive restraint after a costly season because fecundity is structurally one-child-per-success. Intervention: replace the boolean reproduction gate with a reproductive allocation model that chooses offspring count and per-offspring investment under explicit energy budgets, then expose those allocations to mutation and selection. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.
- [expand] [Space Without Exclusion] The world stores occupancy as integer counts per cell, movement and settlement always choose among admissible coordinates without capacity checks, and cells can accumulate arbitrary co-occupancy before same-cell encounters resolve. That means the system cannot express territorial exclusion, shelter monopolization, crowding-driven blocking fronts, body-size-limited packing, or habitat engineering that works by denying physical access; space is only a weighted count field, not a contested substrate with admission constraints. Intervention: add per-cell occupancy capacity or footprint slots plus movement and settlement admission rules, and let contests for occupied sites depend on size, defense, or prior residency instead of only post-hoc co-occupancy encounters. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Globally Synchronous Forcing] `seasonalRegenMultiplierForTick()` and `seasonalFertilityContrastMultiplierForTick()` apply one shared sinusoidal phase to the whole map, while disturbance timing is also a single global schedule. Under this framework the environment can vary in magnitude but not in local timing, drift, or regime identity, so lineages cannot face asynchronous seasons, moving climate fronts, regional bad years, or persistent source-sink mosaics driven by out-of-phase forcing. Intervention: promote forcing into spatial fields with per-cell or per-region phase, amplitude, trend, and stochastic components, then feed those local signals into resource regeneration, fertility, disturbance, and agent observability. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.
