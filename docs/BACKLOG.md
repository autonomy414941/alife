## Critic

- [critic] [Physical Exclusion] Cells have no hard occupancy limit or ownership state: any number of agents can stack in one location, and crowding only enters through soft scoring or transfer modifiers. Within that geometry the system cannot express territory, shelter monopolization, sessile takeover, or local carrying capacity enforced by blocked settlement. Intervention: add per-cell capacity or ownership state plus settlement / encounter rules that can deny entry, evict incumbents, or reserve space. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [critic] [Life-History Compression] `Agent` state only carries position, energy, age, lineage / species ids, and genome, and age is only used as a death cap. No amount of Planner tuning can produce juveniles, adults, dormant propagules, gestation, ontogenetic niche shifts, or reproductive schedules because there are no stage-specific states or transitions to evolve. Intervention: add explicit life stages and reproductive state with stage-specific behavior, mortality, and transition timing. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [critic] [Fixed Phase Ordering] Every tick is hard-wired to run resource regeneration, disturbance, adult turns, same-cell encounters, reproduction, then death / recycling in that order. That architecture cannot express mechanisms whose effect depends on interleaving or reordering events, such as movement interrupted by combat, newborns that immediately compete, or hazards that strike after settlement but before reproduction. Intervention: replace the monolithic step pipeline with a configurable event scheduler or pluggable stage order for movement, encounters, reproduction, mortality, and recycling. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [critic] [Horizon Scalability] The simulator appends per-tick `timeline` entries for every taxon and stores a full `localityFrames` snapshot for every tick, while the relabel-null studies replay those histories end to end. That means run horizon, world size, and taxon count are jointly capped by memory and postprocessing cost, so the current framework cannot probe genuinely long-run open-ended regimes or much larger arenas no matter how well the Planner tunes within it. Intervention: replace always-on full histories with streaming aggregates, sparse event logs, and opt-in checkpoints sufficient for null reconstruction. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [critic] [Species-Conditioned Null] `buildMatchedSchedulePseudoClades()` constructs the matched null by repartitioning the actual `species` histories, so any intervention that increases species origination timing, persistence, or abundance is inherited by both the actual and null arms. Within that evaluation loop, the headline clade-vs-null delta cannot distinguish "opened more upstream innovation opportunities" from "reassigned the same realized species substrate into clades." Intervention: add a decomposition panel with a second null that does not condition on realized species histories, and report upstream species-generation gains separately from clade-structuring gains given the realized species substrate. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [critic] [Uniform Agent Clock] `step()` gives every surviving agent exactly one adult turn, one encounter phase, and one reproduction check per tick, with metabolism and aging advanced on that same shared cadence. Under that scheduler the system cannot express fast-versus-slow taxa, bursty actors, reaction-time asymmetries, or other tempo-based ecological strategies, because all lineages live on one hard-coded action clock even before life stages are considered. Intervention: replace the all-agents-once-per-tick sweep with per-agent action clocks or an event queue that decouples movement, encounter, reproduction, metabolism, and aging rates. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [critic] [Stationary Forcing Family] Environmental forcing is confined to a single sinusoidal seasonal wave plus fixed-interval disturbance shocks with static loss and footprint parameters. No amount of optimization inside that family can generate drifting climates, clustered extremes, multi-scale seasonality, or endogenous hazard regimes, so the system cannot test whether its diversification mechanisms survive nonstationary or feedback-coupled worlds. Intervention: replace the hard-coded forcing formulas with pluggable forcing generators that emit realized climate and disturbance trajectories and support trend, autocorrelation, multi-frequency, and endogenous-trigger variants. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [critic] [Founder-Context Compression] `TaxonFounderContext` collapses every taxon's founding conditions to `habitatMean`, `localCrowdingMean`, coarse bins, and `founderCount`, and `buildMatchedSchedulePseudoClades()` carries only that compressed summary forward. Within this framework, a clade founded by one crowded burst and a clade founded by many dispersed pioneers across heterogeneous sites can become indistinguishable to the null and downstream analysis if their means land in the same bins. Intervention: replace the single centroid summary with a lightweight founder-event sketch such as per-birth samples or histogrammed habitat / crowding / disturbance tuples so null families can preserve founder-context distributions rather than only averages. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [critic] [Spatial Range Blindspot] The simulator records global `localityFrames` dominated by species ids, but the study surface exports no per-species or per-clade range size, fragmentation, overlap, or boundary metrics. That means two runs with the same persistence and occupancy-weighted activity can score identically even if one produces stable segregated ranges and the other is just diffuse mixing, so geography-driven diversification or coexistence gains remain largely invisible to selection. Intervention: add per-taxon spatial footprint summaries and time series, including occupied-area, connected-component count, patch turnover, and pairwise range-overlap metrics, and score interventions on those alongside persistence. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [critic] [Phenotypic Plasticity Ceiling] After birth, an `Agent` only changes `energy` and `age`; ecologically relevant behavior is mediated by taxon-level habitat / trophic / defense summaries and founder-genome lookups rather than mutable individual phenotype. No amount of tuning inside that architecture can produce acclimation, inducible defense, facultative trophic shifts, or reversible habitat switching within a lineage because there is no post-birth phenotype state or reaction norm. Intervention: add mutable per-agent phenotype state plus heritable reaction-norm parameters that update from local conditions and feed back into harvest, encounter, movement, and settlement decisions. Dimension: Expressiveness.
