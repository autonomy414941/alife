Intervention: compare policy-trait-space richness against action-signature, exposure-conditioned, or descendant-success metrics to see whether the current signal is ecologically meaningful.

- [validate] [Matched-Null Fidelity] Relabel-null baselines still ignore founder habitat, crowding, disturbance context, and policy state. Intervention: add stricter matched-null families before treating delta-vs-null gains as evidence.
- [expand] [Policy-Payoff Reach Ceiling] Harvest intake and reserve spending now read policy state, but encounter transfer, settlement scoring, and metabolic burden still remain morphology-dominated. Intervention: extend direct policy payoff coupling to at least one additional demographic operator and compare marginal gain against the current harvest/reserve-only coupling.
- [expand] [Environmental Complexity] The world is still a static fertility map with shallow disturbance and no persistent biotic construction. Intervention: add long-lived per-cell state layers that agents deposit, erode, and sense.
- [expand] [One-Dimensional Habitat Axis] Habitat remains a single fertility scalar, preventing orthogonal abiotic tradeoffs. Intervention: replace the single field with a vector of abiotic layers and multi-axis tolerance phenotypes.
- [expand] [Exogenous Regime Ceiling] Large-scale regimes are still imposed from outside the biota. Intervention: make regeneration, fertility contrast, and disturbance propensity depend on accumulated biotic state or persistent field layers.
- [expand] [Observability Blind Spots] Agents still lack direct access to age, disturbance history, encounter outcomes, local taxonomic composition, and seasonal or abiotic signals. Intervention: populate a read-only observation map and expose it to policy logic and analytics.
- [expand] [Memory Horizon] Behavioral control now has multi-tick rolling and decay-weighted memory features for harvest, but other decision inputs (crowding, fertility, reserve state) remain single-tick. Intervention: extend rolling/decay memory to all major decision surfaces and test whether richer temporal context improves policy-bearing cohorts under matched controls.
- [refactor] [Trait Granularity] Habitat preference, trophic level, and defense remain partly mediated through taxon-level summaries and caches rather than fully per-agent expressed phenotype. Intervention: derive ecological summaries from per-agent phenotype distributions instead of privileged taxon caches.
- [expand] [Interaction Richness] The interaction alphabet is still fixed to movement, harvest, same-cell theft, reproduction, and thresholded clade founding. Intervention: generalize encounters and foraging into evolvable action channels.
- [expand] [Inheritance Architecture] Reproduction remains single-parent clonal mutation with threshold side effects. Intervention: add multi-parent inheritance or exchange operators and explicit compatibility rules.
- [expand] [Evolutionary Mechanisms] Speciation and cladogenesis still occur in a fully connected torus without barriers or sustained isolation. Intervention: add patch barriers or patch graphs plus lineage-local breeding structure.
- [expand] [Finite Locus Catalog] New loci can only be drawn from a fixed human-authored candidate list. Intervention: replace fixed catalogs with a trait-module factory or parameterized locus generator.
- [expand] [Direct-Encoding Ceiling] All traits are still expressed as genotype scalars with no explicit context-dependent phenotype construction stage. Intervention: add an intermediate phenotype layer mapping `(genomeV2, local context) -> expressed traits`.
- [validate] [Unbounded Trait Accumulation] Presence-activated and unbounded policy traits can still accumulate distance without guaranteed ecological effect. Intervention: add activation costs, pruning, or range-aware normalization beyond the current category weights.
- [validate] [RNG Sequence Non-Reproducibility] Small architectural changes still alter RNG schedules and destroy comparability. Intervention: isolate RNG streams by mechanism or add deterministic checkpoint branching.
- [expand] [Abiotic-Only Food-Web Ceiling] `resource` and `resource2` are passive abiotic stocks with exogenous regeneration and decomposition return, while `trophic_level` and `aggression` only modulate harvest penalties or same-cell energy theft. Predator-prey cycles, producer guilds, and trophic cascades are therefore structurally impossible no matter how the Planner tunes parameters. Intervention: add biotic producer or prey pools with their own birth/death dynamics and couple trophic traits directly to those living resource flows. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Thresholded Taxonomy Ceiling] Species and clades still appear the moment parent-child genome distance crosses `speciationThreshold` or `cladogenesisThreshold`, with no provisional split state, persistence requirement, or ecological separation test. The system therefore cannot express gradual speciation, failed incipient taxa, or temporary hybridizing branches within the current framework. Intervention: add staged taxon formation where candidate splits must maintain spatial or ecological separation for a configurable window before promotion. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [expand] [Action-Encoding Ceiling] Agents currently have a fixed behavioral repertoire: move or stay, harvest, reproduce if threshold met, encounter transfer. All variation comes through continuous trait modulation of these operations, not through discrete action selection or sequencing. The system therefore cannot express conditional multi-step strategies ("if harvest failed, then move toward higher fertility"), state-dependent action switching ("enter defensive mode when energy drops below reserve"), or action inhibition beyond the current binary gates. Even graded policies only modulate single-decision probabilities, not action sequences or fallback chains. Intervention: replace the current single-turn action model with an action selection layer where agents choose from a discrete action alphabet, and policy loci determine action priorities, thresholds, and fallback rules. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Synchronous Time Ceiling] All agents act in a single sequential pass within each tick, with no sub-tick ordering randomization or event-driven scheduling. This creates artificial simultaneity: an agent that would have died from energy depletion can still reproduce and harvest during the same tick, and spatial race conditions are determined purely by iteration order rather than true temporal precedence. The system cannot express temporal niche separation, rapid-response behaviors, or asynchronous encounter cascades where one death triggers immediate local vacancy competition. Intervention: replace the fixed-order tick loop with an event queue where actions have explicit durations or priority, and agents act asynchronously based on readiness timers, or randomize agent order within each tick. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [validate] [Within-Species Phenotype Compression] `summarizeTraitSpaceDiversity` first averages trait values per species and then bins only those species centroids into niches, so stable polymorphisms, geographically split ecotypes, and other within-species phenotype structure collapse to a single point. The feedback layer therefore cannot detect diversification that remains below the current taxonomic threshold even when it is ecologically real. Intervention: add agent-level and lineage-level niche occupancy metrics plus within-species phenotype-dispersion summaries, then compare them against the current species-centroid metric. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [expand] [One-Step Dispersal Ceiling] Adult movement and offspring settlement can only choose among the current cell and the four cardinal neighbors, while `dispersalRadius` only changes crowding calculations. Rare long-distance colonization, evolvable dispersal kernels, and phenotype-specific movement range are therefore impossible within the current action model no matter how parameters are tuned. Intervention: separate movement and settlement reach from the crowding radius, then allow configurable or evolvable multi-cell candidate sets with explicit distance costs. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Shared Spatial Utility Ceiling] `pickDestination` and offspring settlement both optimize the same immediate `resolveSettlementEcologyScore` scalar `food - crowding - lineage penalty + jitter`. The architecture therefore assumes adults and juveniles value space identically and only on the current tick, making stage-specific habitat choice, exploratory movement for deferred payoff, and dispersal-establishment tradeoffs structurally unreachable. Intervention: split spatial decision evaluation into distinct adult-movement and juvenile-establishment utilities, with phenotype or policy traits controlling their weights and temporal horizon. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [expand] [Ontogeny Ceiling] `age` currently changes survival only through `maxAge`, while metabolism, harvest, movement, encounter transfer, and reproduction all use the same phenotype and operator weights from birth onward. The system therefore cannot express maturation delay, juvenile-adult niche partitioning, senescence tradeoffs, or stage-specific reproductive strategies no matter how the Planner tunes parameters. Intervention: add explicit life stages or age-conditioned phenotype realization so action rules, costs, and fecundity can vary across development. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Perfect-Information Spatial Choice Ceiling] Adult movement and offspring settlement score candidate cells from ground-truth resource stocks, crowding, habitat match, and lineage occupancy rather than from limited sensed observations. The architecture therefore cannot express exploration under uncertainty, sensory error, deceptive cues, or evolution of perception quality within the current framework. Intervention: insert a perception layer that exposes noisy or partial observations and drive spatial choice from perceived state instead of direct environment access. Dimension: Assumptions.
- [expand] [Independent-Locus Genetics Ceiling] `GenomeV2` remains a flat map of scalar loci, mutation perturbs each trait independently, and phenotype realization mostly reads each key directly. Coordinated gene complexes, epistasis, linkage drag, and sign-switching genetic effects are therefore structurally out of reach even if more loci are added. Intervention: introduce regulatory or module-level loci whose effects depend on other loci or shared module state, then update mutation and distance/speciation logic to respect those dependencies. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [validate] [Ancestry Horizon Ceiling] `SimulationEvolutionHistory` retains only the most recent 2048 `descentEdges`, so long-run descendant attribution and multi-generation innovation persistence disappear once runs exceed that buffer. The feedback layer therefore cannot verify whether a policy or phenotype change produces durable lineage effects beyond the recent ancestry window. Intervention: replace the fixed-size edge buffer with a compressed persistent ancestry index or periodic lineage summaries that preserve descendant outcomes across the full run. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [validate] [Policy-Cohort Collapse Ceiling] The April 1 signature validation identified one signature (`open|strict|balanced`) with positive harvest advantage, but the taxonomy still reduces policy state to three ternary axes (9 bins), pooling finer-grained threshold and preference variation. Intervention: add continuous-state nearest-neighbor matching or replay-branch counterfactuals to compare identical policy states instead of coarse signature bins.
- [expand] [Fixed Resource-Channel Ceiling] The environment and body still hardcode exactly two substrate channels: `resource`/`resource2`, `maxResource`/`maxResource2`, `energyPrimary`/`energySecondary`, and paired metabolic-efficiency and preference traits. The system therefore cannot express third-resource limitation, multi-nutrient stoichiometry, or guilds specialized on more than two interchangeable currencies no matter how the Planner tunes parameters. Intervention: replace the duplicated primary/secondary fields with indexed resource vectors and per-channel uptake, storage, and spending traits so simulations can scale beyond a hand-authored pair. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [validate] [One-Tick Policy Credit Ceiling] `PolicyFitnessRecord` currently scores each exposure only by same-tick `harvestIntake`, `survived`, and `offspringProduced`. Delayed consequences of movement, reserve spending, settlement context, or cautious reproduction are therefore mostly invisible to the feedback layer, which can optimize short-term intake proxies while missing policies whose payoff arrives several ticks later. Intervention: add multi-horizon decision attribution, such as +5/+20/+50 tick survival and descendant windows, or replay-branch counterfactual scoring from captured states. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
- [validate] [Coarse Context Matching Ceiling] Policy-fitness matching still bins decision context into only `fertilityBin`, `crowdingBin`, `ageBin`, and a binary `disturbancePhase`, while ignoring resource-layer mix, habitat mismatch, local lineage composition, disturbance-opening state, and the agent's exact policy thresholds. Even after policy-signature stratification, context-conditional advantages can remain structurally hidden because behaviorally different exposures are treated as matched. Intervention: replace the fixed coarse bins with richer decision-state matching, such as higher-dimensional context keys, nearest-neighbor matching over full local state, or replay-branch comparisons from identical captured contexts. Dimension: Feedback Loops.
