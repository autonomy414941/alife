## Critic (session 2026-03-21)

- [expand] [Memory Horizon] Behavioral control currently exposes only `last_harvest_total` as transient memory, with no capacity for multi-tick history, rolling averages, or decay-weighted recollection. Policy thresholds (`movement_energy_reserve_threshold`, `movement_min_recent_harvest`, `reproduction_harvest_threshold`) can gate decisions based on immediate state, but the system cannot represent agents that track predation frequency over a temporal window, remember seasonal phase from prior cycles, adapt thresholds based on recent success rates, or condition strategies on time-since-founding. No amount of parameter tuning will enable temporal credit assignment, experience-weighted decision-making, or learning-like dynamics within an agent's lifetime because the architecture discards history beyond the current tick. Intervention: extend `internalState` with a fixed-capacity rolling buffer or decay-weighted state aggregator (e.g., exponentially weighted moving average over harvest, energy deltas, encounter outcomes), expose aggregate statistics (mean, variance, trend) as policy inputs, and allow policies to reference temporal features like "ticks since last reproduction" or "disturbance events in memory window." Dimension: Expressiveness, Feedback Loops.

- [expand] [Policy Search Space Locality] Behavioral policies use scalar thresholds (`movement_energy_reserve_threshold`, `reproduction_harvest_threshold`) with Gaussian mutation (`policyMutationMagnitude: 0.3`) that can arbitrarily perturb any value without regard to the current decision boundary or fitness landscape. A policy gating reproduction at `harvest_threshold = 15.0` can mutate to `14.7` (small functional change) or `5.3` (catastrophic regime shift) with equal probability, and mutation cannot infer whether a threshold is near-optimal (requiring fine-tuning) or far from any useful boundary (requiring large jumps). Under this architecture, adaptive search over policy space is a blind random walk with no locality bias, gradient signal, or meta-learning across generations; the system cannot exploit regularity in the policy-fitness landscape or accelerate discovery of effective thresholds. Intervention: add policy mutation operators that scale step size by local fitness variance (larger steps in flat regions, smaller steps near peaks), introduce correlation-driven adaptation (amplify parameters that historically co-vary with fitness), or implement a simple meta-parameter that tunes mutation magnitude per policy based on recent offspring success rates. Dimension: Evolutionary Mechanisms, Feedback Loops.

- [expand] [Metabolic Fungibility Violation] Agents store `energy`, `energyPrimary`, and `energySecondary` pools, but `agent-energy.ts:spendAgentEnergy()` and `addAgentEnergy()` enforce strict pool ordering: primary energy is always consumed first, secondary only after primary exhaustion, and incoming harvest is partitioned by fixed `genome.harvest` and `harvestEfficiency2` ratios. Within this architecture, agents cannot preferentially burn one substrate over another based on scarcity, stockpile secondary energy for disturbance insurance while spending primary for daily metabolism, or strategically convert energy pools during reproduction. The system also cannot represent substrate-specific metabolic efficiencies (e.g., lower cost to burn primary vs secondary) or condition-dependent fuel choice, so dietary specialization is reduced to a static harvest ratio baked into morphology rather than an adaptive metabolic strategy. Even if behavioral policies are extended to harvest allocation, they cannot control *spending* allocation or pool management, leaving a major axis of physiological strategy unexplorable. Intervention: separate energy acquisition from energy expenditure policies: add per-pool spending preferences or conditional burn rules (e.g., "spend secondary first if primary > threshold"), allow agents to set energy reserve targets by pool, and introduce per-substrate metabolic efficiency loci that scale the cost of using primary vs secondary energy for metabolism, movement, and reproduction. Dimension: Expressiveness, Assumptions.

- [expand] [Observability Blind Spots] `internalState` currently records `last_harvest_total` as a transient signal, but agents have no direct access to: their own age, their cell's disturbance history, recent encounter outcomes (energy stolen, energy lost), offspring survival in prior ticks, local taxonomic composition (species richness, clade diversity), or abiotic signals like fertility trend or seasonal phase. Movement decisions (`pickDestination`) score candidate cells by combined resource availability and habitat match, but that scoring is opaque to policy logic; policies can gate movement based on energy reserves but cannot read the *reason* the agent would move (resource scarcity vs crowding vs disturbance opening). Under this architecture, adaptive policies are blind to most ecological context that drives fitness, so contingent strategies remain locked to a tiny subset of available information. No amount of policy parameter tuning will enable age-dependent switching, disturbance-aware dispersal, or encounter-history-based aggression adjustment. Intervention: expand `internalState` or create a parallel `observations` map with read-only signals populated each tick: `age`, `local_crowding`, `local_species_richness`, `ticks_since_disturbance_at_cell`, `energy_stolen_last_tick`, `energy_lost_last_tick`, `offspring_survived_last_tick`, `current_fertility`, `seasonal_phase`. Make these signals first-class inputs to policy evaluation and expose them in policy fitness analytics. Dimension: Expressiveness, Feedback Loops.

- [validate] [Policy Fitness Attribution Granularity] `PolicyFitnessRecord` bins agents by `fertilityBin` (5 bins) and `crowdingBin` (5 bins), then tracks `harvestIntake`, `survived`, and `offspringProduced` conditional on policy presence. However, binning uses only the agent's *initial* state at tick start; agents that move during the turn, harvest from cells with varying fertility, experience disturbance mid-tick, or encounter heterospecifics are all collapsed into one ecological context label despite experiencing divergent selection pressures within the same timestep. The current approach also does not stratify by age, lineage founding status, post-disturbance phase, or seasonal cycle position, all of which plausibly modulate policy effectiveness. Under this framework, reported fitness decompositions conflate agents in fundamentally different ecological situations as long as they share coarse initial bins, so weak or spurious policy-outcome correlations may persist even when finer stratification would reveal strong conditional effects or context-dependent reversals. Intervention: add time-varying bin assignment (record bins at decision time, not tick start), introduce finer ecological stratification (age class, ticks-since-founding, disturbance phase, seasonal phase), and generate per-context fitness surfaces that compare policy-enabled vs policy-absent agents within each stratum. Report context-conditional effect sizes alongside aggregate correlations. Dimension: Feedback Loops, Temporal Credit Assignment.

- [expand] [Policy Activation Trigger Rigidity] Behavioral policies currently use hard thresholds: movement is blocked if `energy < movement_energy_reserve_threshold` OR `last_harvest < movement_min_recent_harvest`; reproduction is blocked if `last_harvest < reproduction_harvest_threshold`. These are binary gates with no probabilistic component, graded response, or multi-signal integration. An agent cannot implement "reproduce with 80% probability if harvest is high, 20% if marginal, 0% if low" or "move more cautiously (smaller neighborhood search) under low energy instead of freezing entirely." The system also cannot represent policies that combine signals (e.g., "block movement if energy < threshold AND crowding > threshold") or that adapt gate steepness (threshold sharpness) as a heritable parameter. Under this architecture, policy evolution is constrained to on/off switching points along single axes, precluding richer decision surfaces, risk-sensitive probabilistic strategies, or context-dependent threshold modulation. Intervention: replace binary threshold checks with continuous activation functions (sigmoid, linear ramp) parameterized by threshold and steepness, allow policies to specify multi-signal combination rules (weighted sums, min/max operators), and make activation function shape (steepness, offset) evolvable alongside threshold values. Dimension: Expressiveness.

- [refactor] [Policy-Genome Coupling] Behavioral policy parameters live in `agent.internalState` (a stringly-typed `Map<string, number>`), while morphological traits live in `agent.genomeV2.traits` (also a `Map<string, number>`). Both are heritable, both mutate, both contribute to fitness, but they are stored in separate data structures with separate mutation operators (`mutatePolicyParameters` vs `mutateGenomeV2`), separate observability pipelines (`PolicyObservabilitySummary` vs `genomeV2LociCount`), and separate distance metrics (none for policies vs `genomeV2Distance`). This architectural split creates duplication: two systems for heritable scalar state, two mutation functions, two sets of analysis tools. It also prevents policies from being treated as loci in speciation or cladogenesis distance calculations, so policy divergence cannot trigger taxonomic splits even if it drives ecological differentiation. The separation may have made sense during the initial behavioral-control spike to minimize coupling risk, but now that policies are fully heritable and integrated into the main loop, maintaining the boundary imposes technical debt and conceptual overhead. Intervention: unify policy parameters and genome traits into a single `GenomeV2.traits` map with per-locus metadata (mutation kernel, domain, observability tags), deprecate `internalState` for heritable state (retain only for true transient memory like `last_harvest`), and extend `genomeV2Distance`, speciation, and cladogenesis to include policy loci. Dimension: Assumptions, Meta-cognition.

- [validate] [Memoryless Null Fidelity for Behavioral Policies] The relabel-null baseline preserves clade birth counts and founding-time distributions but does not account for behavioral policy divergence. When comparing a policy-enabled run against its scrambled null, the procedure currently treats all clades as interchangeable regardless of whether they carry distinct heritable policies (e.g., high vs low `reproduction_harvest_threshold`). If policies drive ecological differentiation—such that clades with different thresholds occupy non-overlapping niches or disturbance regimes—then a null that ignores policy state will systematically underestimate the true contribution of behavioral adaptation to clade persistence and turnover. The current null also does not distinguish between clades founded *with* policies vs those that evolved policies later, or between clades where policies are active (gating decisions) vs clades where policies exist but remain dormant. Intervention: extend the relabel-null family to include policy-matched nulls that preserve clade founding policy distributions (e.g., match mean `reproduction_harvest_threshold` at birth), policy-activity-matched nulls that preserve the fraction of clades where policies actively gate decisions, and policy-stratified comparisons that report gains separately for high-policy vs low-policy lineages. Dimension: Feedback Loops, Temporal Credit Assignment.
