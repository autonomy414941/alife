- [expand] [Behavioral Control] Agent decisions are memoryless and hard-coded: movement and offspring settlement greedily maximize a one-step local ecology score over five cells, encounters always resolve through the most aggressive occupant, and reproduction is only an energy threshold plus probability gate. No amount of parameter tuning can produce contingent strategies such as dormancy, scouting, pursuit / escape, seasonal migration, retreat-after-loss, or partner choice because agents have no mutable internal state or evolvable controller. Intervention: add per-agent internal state and an evolvable policy layer that maps observations and recent history to movement, harvest, encounter, and reproduction decisions.
- [expand] [Inheritance Architecture] Every descendant is created by single-parent clonal mutation, with speciation and cladogenesis as irreversible threshold side effects of that one lineage. Within this framework the system cannot express recombination, sexual selection, mating compatibility, or horizontal trait transfer, so innovation is limited to local mutation walks around one parent genome. Intervention: introduce multi-parent inheritance or exchange operators plus compatibility / partner-choice rules, and make parentage an explicit part of descendant creation.
- [expand] [Interaction Richness] The interaction alphabet is compile-time fixed to movement, harvest, same-cell energy theft, reproduction, and thresholded clade founding, so lineages cannot evolve new interaction types such as signaling, cooperation, public goods, or alternative predation / resource-conversion modes. Intervention: generalize encounters and foraging into evolvable action channels over multiple resource or field layers instead of a single hard-coded transfer equation.
- [expand] [Environmental Complexity] The world is a static fertility map plus one fungible resource pool with exogenous disturbance; agents can deplete and recycle resources but cannot persistently construct, poison, shelter, or otherwise rewrite habitat. Intervention: add persistent per-cell state layers that agents deposit, erode, and sense over many ticks, then feed those layers back into movement, harvest, and survival.
- [expand] [Evolutionary Mechanisms] Speciation and cladogenesis are single-offspring threshold events in a wrapped torus with no barriers, demes, mate choice, or lineage-specific breeding structure, so the system cannot express allopatric isolation, reproductive barriers, or adaptive radiation driven by sustained separation. Intervention: add patch barriers or a patch graph plus lineage-local colonization / breeding pools, and require sustained ecological or spatial separation before promoting a new clade.
- [feat] [Descent Observability] `TaxonHistory` retains only per-taxon counts and timelines; the simulation discards parent-child edges, founder trait snapshots beyond a clade founder genome, and phenotype transitions at speciation / cladogenesis time. That makes cumulative novelty, convergence, evolutionary dead ends, and innovation reuse structurally unmeasurable no matter how the Planner reshapes current studies. Intervention: persist a lightweight genealogy / innovation graph with parent ids, founder traits, and phenotype deltas at taxon birth events, then derive novelty and reuse metrics from that graph.
- [validate] [Temporal Credit Assignment] The study surface mostly aggregates final snapshots, fixed-window turnover, and persistence-threshold deltas, so interventions that create repeated but short-lived innovations or long transient scaffolding before late collapse are nearly invisible to the current optimization loop. Under the present framework the system can optimize endpoint persistence proxies, but not ratcheting search over trajectory quality. Intervention: add first-class trajectory metrics such as area under active-clade diversity, innovation survival curves, regime-switch counts, and recolonization-time distributions alongside the existing final-state summaries.
- [refactor] [Trait Granularity] Habitat preference, trophic level, and defense level live in taxon-level scalar maps and are only updated on speciation or clade founding, while movement, harvest, and encounters consume those taxon summaries instead of individual phenotype. That makes within-species ecological polymorphism, frequency-dependent morph coexistence, and gradual niche divergence inside an extant species structurally impossible; ecological differentiation only becomes visible after a taxonomic split. Intervention: move ecological phenotype fields to heritable per-agent state updated every birth, and derive species / clade summaries from those distributions instead of using taxon-level lookup tables as the source of truth.
- [expand] [Dispersal Kinematics] Adults can only stay put or move one cardinal step per tick, and offspring can only settle in the parent cell or its four cardinal neighbors; `dispersalRadius` changes crowding calculations, not move or settlement distance. No amount of parameter tuning can produce rare long-range colonization, dispersal polymorphisms, migration fronts with variable jump lengths, or metapopulation rescue via occasional distant propagules. Intervention: separate sensing radius from movement / settlement kernels, then add configurable or evolvable step-length and offspring-dispersal distributions including rare long-distance events.
- [validate] [Taxonomic Proxy Leakage] The optimization surface still scores success mainly through species / clade counts, persistence windows, turnover, and relabel-null deltas, but taxon identities are minted by threshold crossings rather than by demonstrated ecological distinctness. That means the Planner can win on paper through ecologically near-identical taxonomic splits or by preserving labels without opening new niches, and the current summaries will often treat that as diversification. Intervention: add ecology-weighted diversity metrics that discount near-duplicate taxa, such as phenotype-distance-weighted richness, effective niche occupancy, and interaction-distinct clade counts, and make those co-equal with raw taxon counts in study decisions.
- [validate] [Matched-Null Fidelity] The relabel-null baseline preserves clade birth counts by tick, then randomly reassigns species into pseudo-clades without preserving founder habitat, local crowding, abundance class, or disturbance context. Under that proxy, a stack can look better than null while still failing to create genuinely distinct ecological opportunities. Intervention: add stricter null families that also match founder habitat bin, local occupancy / lineage crowding, abundance class, and disturbance phase at birth before treating delta-vs-null gains as evidence of progress.
- [feat] [Mechanistic Causality] `TaxonHistory` tracks births, deaths, and population counts, but the system does not retain cause-specific losses, harvest intake, predation transfers, movement costs, or disturbance impacts by species / clade. That makes it structurally impossible to tell whether a reported gain came from better founding, lower same-lineage crowding, reduced predation, or simple resource hoarding. Intervention: add per-taxon demographic and energy ledgers with cause tags, then surface founder-phase and maintenance-phase budgets in the study exports.
- [validate] [Absolute-Versus-Null Fixation] The optimization loop credits interventions for improving delta-versus-null (founder grace +9 vs static habitat) while ignoring that both arms sit far below zero in absolute active clades (founder grace -25.75, static habitat -34.75). Under that objective surface the Planner can win on relative gains while the system as a whole continues losing to the null baseline, which means reported progress does not necessarily mean the system is approaching genuinely positive diversification. Intervention: add co-equal absolute active-clade targets or minimum viability floors alongside delta metrics, and surface both "better than the alternative" and "better than null" as distinct success criteria.
- [validate] [Persistence-Window Myopia] `collectTaxonActivityContext()` and the persistent-activity studies credit taxa only for ticks where they are actively occupied within the evaluation window, which makes slowly-establishing taxa, delayed-expansion lineages, and taxa that matter through later descendant founding structurally invisible to the headline metrics even if they ultimately drive diversification. Within that truncation the system optimizes for immediate occupancy bursts instead of delayed-payoff founding chains or slow niche construction. Intervention: add cumulative-lineage-contribution metrics that attribute downstream founding, long-run occupancy beyond the origin window, and multi-generation impact chains across the full run horizon instead of only scoring origin-window activity.
- [strategize] [Single-Mechanism Tuning Ceiling] The current research surface sweeps numeric and boolean fields in `SimulationConfig` (founder grace ticks, crowding penalties, clade coupling scalars), while the architecture remains locked to one resource pool, three genome axes, greedy one-step movement, dominant-only encounters, and clonal inheritance. No amount of Planner optimization inside that parameter surface can discover whether the system needs multi-resource constraints, interaction-type diversity, recombination, or alternative encounter resolutions because those mechanism classes are not knobs to turn. Intervention: replace the axis-specific sweep wrappers with composable mechanism slots for resources, inheritance, encounters, and settlement so studies can compare whole operator families instead of only retuning single-mechanism constants.
- [expand] [Reproduction Energy Transfer Simplicity] `reproduceInSimulation()` creates exactly one offspring per successful reproduction event, always transfers exactly `offspringEnergyFraction * parentEnergy` into that single child, and decrements the parent's unified `energy` scalar by the same amount. This architecture cannot express variable litter sizes, differential offspring provisioning, or energy-allocation strategies where parents split energy unevenly among multiple offspring, or where offspring cost depends on environmental conditions or parent state beyond a fixed fraction. Combined with fungible internal energy, reproduction becomes a single-dimensional scalar transfer with no opportunity for offspring-quality versus offspring-quantity tradeoffs, no clutch-composition strategies, and no structured allocation of different internal resource pools to different offspring roles (e.g., dispersers versus competitors). Within this ceiling, life-history diversity is limited to threshold-and-probability gates on when to reproduce, not how to allocate reproductive investment. Intervention: replace single-child fixed-fraction reproduction with a variable-offspring allocation operator that can emit zero to many offspring per event, each with explicit energy provisioning decided by heritable parameters and local conditions, and allow reproduction cost to draw differentially from structured internal resource pools if those are introduced.
- [validate] [Abundance-Metric Fragmentation] The March 15 commit added abundance-weighted activity metrics, but the Planner's optimization loop still primarily targets occupancy-based persistence deltas inherited from earlier studies. As long as the headline objective remains occupancy-only, interventions can win by barely-alive label persistence instead of ecologically consequential expansion, and the new abundance signals remain secondary diagnostics rather than co-equal optimization targets. Intervention: promote abundance-weighted and energy-weighted activity metrics to first-class study objectives alongside occupancy-based persistence, and require interventions to hold up on all three channels before claiming diversification gains.
- [expand] [Physical Exclusion] Cells have no hard occupancy limit or ownership state: any number of agents can stack in one location, and crowding only enters through soft scoring or transfer modifiers. Within that geometry the system cannot express territory, shelter monopolization, sessile takeover, or local carrying capacity enforced by blocked settlement. Intervention: add per-cell capacity or ownership state plus settlement / encounter rules that can deny entry, evict incumbents, or reserve space.
- [expand] [Life-History Compression] `Agent` state only carries position, energy, age, lineage / species ids, and genome, and age is only used as a death cap. No amount of Planner tuning can produce juveniles, adults, dormant propagules, gestation, ontogenetic niche shifts, or reproductive schedules because there are no stage-specific states or transitions to evolve. Intervention: add explicit life stages and reproductive state with stage-specific behavior, mortality, and transition timing.
- [refactor] [Fixed Phase Ordering] Every tick is hard-wired to run resource regeneration, disturbance, adult turns, same-cell encounters, reproduction, then death / recycling in that order. That architecture cannot express mechanisms whose effect depends on interleaving or reordering events, such as movement interrupted by combat, newborns that immediately compete, or hazards that strike after settlement but before reproduction. Intervention: replace the monolithic step pipeline with a configurable event scheduler or pluggable stage order for movement, encounters, reproduction, mortality, and recycling.
- [expand] [Uniform Agent Clock] `step()` gives every surviving agent exactly one adult turn, one encounter phase, and one reproduction check per tick, with metabolism and aging advanced on that same shared cadence. Under that scheduler the system cannot express fast-versus-slow taxa, bursty actors, reaction-time asymmetries, or other tempo-based ecological strategies, because all lineages live on one hard-coded action clock even before life stages are considered. Intervention: replace the all-agents-once-per-tick sweep with per-agent action clocks or an event queue that decouples movement, encounter, reproduction, metabolism, and aging rates.
- [expand] [Stationary Forcing Family] Environmental forcing is confined to a single sinusoidal seasonal wave plus fixed-interval disturbance shocks with static loss and footprint parameters. No amount of optimization inside that family can generate drifting climates, clustered extremes, multi-scale seasonality, or endogenous hazard regimes, so the system cannot test whether its diversification mechanisms survive nonstationary or feedback-coupled worlds. Intervention: replace the hard-coded forcing formulas with pluggable forcing generators that emit realized climate and disturbance trajectories and support trend, autocorrelation, multi-frequency, and endogenous-trigger variants.
- [refactor] [Founder-Context Compression] `TaxonFounderContext` collapses every taxon's founding conditions to `habitatMean`, `localCrowdingMean`, coarse bins, and `founderCount`, and `buildMatchedSchedulePseudoClades()` carries only that compressed summary forward. Within this framework, a clade founded by one crowded burst and a clade founded by many dispersed pioneers across heterogeneous sites can become indistinguishable to the null and downstream analysis if their means land in the same bins. Intervention: replace the single centroid summary with a lightweight founder-event sketch such as per-birth samples or histogrammed habitat / crowding / disturbance tuples so null families can preserve founder-context distributions rather than only averages.
- [validate] [Spatial Range Blindspot] The simulator records global `localityFrames` dominated by species ids, but the study surface exports no per-species or per-clade range size, fragmentation, overlap, or boundary metrics. That means two runs with the same persistence and occupancy-weighted activity can score identically even if one produces stable segregated ranges and the other is just diffuse mixing, so geography-driven diversification or coexistence gains remain largely invisible to selection. Intervention: add per-taxon spatial footprint summaries and time series, including occupied-area, connected-component count, patch turnover, and pairwise range-overlap metrics, and score interventions on those alongside persistence.
- [expand] [Phenotypic Plasticity Ceiling] After birth, an `Agent` only changes `energy` and `age`; ecologically relevant behavior is mediated by taxon-level habitat / trophic / defense summaries and founder-genome lookups rather than mutable individual phenotype. No amount of tuning inside that architecture can produce acclimation, inducible defense, facultative trophic shifts, or reversible habitat switching within a lineage because there is no post-birth phenotype state or reaction norm. Intervention: add mutable per-agent phenotype state plus heritable reaction-norm parameters that update from local conditions and feed back into harvest, encounter, movement, and settlement decisions.
- [refactor] [Uniform Disturbance Susceptibility] `applyDisturbanceIfScheduled()` chooses a geometric footprint plus random refugia, then applies the same energy-loss multiplier to every affected agent and the same resource-loss multiplier to every affected cell. That means the system cannot represent fire-resistant lineages, habitat-specific shielding, stage-specific mortality, or trait-mediated disturbance exposure; adaptation can only shift where organisms are when shocks land, not how shocks discriminate among them. Intervention: split disturbance into a footprint generator and an impact kernel keyed by agent traits, life stage, habitat layers, and local state, and record selective loss channels separately from footprint size.
- [refactor] [Taxon Irreversibility] Species and clades are minted by `nextSpeciesId++` and `nextLineageId++` and are never merged, aliased, or reclassified after birth, while `TaxonHistory` treats each id as permanently distinct until extinction. Under that state model convergence-driven fusion, lineage assimilation, or dynamic species concepts are structurally impossible, so threshold-created labels can only accumulate or die. Intervention: add explicit taxon parentage plus phenotype- or genealogy-based merge / alias rules, or shift evaluation onto dynamic clusters so near-duplicate taxa can collapse instead of remaining forever distinct ids.
- [refactor] [Founder-Locked Clade Interaction State] `getCladeTrophicLevel()` and `getCladeDefenseLevel()` always derive clade-level interaction traits from `getCladeFounderGenome(lineage)`, while only habitat preference has an adaptive clade memory. As a result, old clades with heavily diverged descendants still project the founder's trophic / defense profile into `cladeInteractionCoupling`, so clade-level interaction niches cannot drift with descendant evolution. Intervention: replace founder-locked clade interaction signals with descendant-weighted clade trait summaries or rolling clade trait distributions updated from living members and births.
- [expand] [Oracle Perception] `pickDestination()` and `pickSettlementSite()` score every candidate cell with direct access to exact resource levels, habitat-match output, and crowding summaries through `localEcologyScore()`, with no sensing range, observation noise, or information cost. Within that control surface the system cannot express perceptual uncertainty, scouting, camouflage, cue exploitation, or tradeoffs between sensing and acting because agents already behave as if they can perfectly inspect the relevant neighborhood for free. Intervention: split latent world state from sensed observations, add evolvable sensor parameters and noisy / costly sampling, and make controllers act on observations rather than direct world queries.
- [expand] [Mutation Topology Ceiling] `mutateGenome()` perturbs `metabolism`, `harvest`, and `aggression` independently with small bounded additive noise, while speciation and cladogenesis only inspect distance within that same tiny continuous cube. No amount of Planner tuning can produce linked mutations, pleiotropic tradeoffs, neutral substructures, rare macro-mutations, or structural innovation jumps because evolutionary search is locked to local independent nudges on three scalars. Intervention: replace the independent scalar mutator with a configurable genotype / mutation layer that supports linked loci, discrete structural edits, and variable step-size mutation operators.
- [feat] [Counterfactual Branching Ceiling] `snapshot()` exports only agents plus headline summaries; it omits resources, RNG state, taxon histories, clade / species trait maps, disturbance state, and there is no restore API for full simulation state. Under that architecture the system cannot fork multiple interventions from the same evolved world or run true paired counterfactuals after a shared burn-in, so study comparisons remain dominated by fresh-run seed variance and early-history divergence. Intervention: add full-state checkpoint serialization and restoration, including RNG, resource grids, trait maps, taxon histories, and disturbance bookkeeping, then support branch-from-checkpoint experiment harnesses.
- [expand] [Spatial Climate Synchrony] Seasonal forcing is a function of tick alone: regeneration and fertility-contrast multipliers vary over time but not over space, so every cell experiences the same phase and amplitude on the same schedule. Within that forcing model the system cannot express migrating climate fronts, asynchronous regional refugia, patch-specific phenology, or range shifts driven by spatially lagged seasons no matter how the Planner retunes current knobs. Intervention: replace the global seasonal scalars with spatially explicit forcing fields or patchwise phase / amplitude maps that evolve through time and feed into per-cell regeneration and fertility.
- [validate] [Community Composition Compression] `LocalityFrame` records only `dominantSpeciesByCell`, dominance share, neighborhood dominance, and richness. That means subordinate composition, evenness, replacement among non-dominant taxa, and stable mixed communities are largely invisible to the feedback loop; two runs can look equivalent if they share the same dominant labels even when their local community structure is radically different. Intervention: retain sparse per-cell composition sketches or derive beta-diversity, evenness, and subdominant-turnover metrics from the live grid, then surface those alongside the current dominance summaries.
- [validate] [Origin-Window Credit Ceiling] `collectTaxonActivityContext()` credits each new species or clade only for `activityInOriginWindow`, and the persistent-activity studies reuse that same origin-window contribution. Under that metric surface, taxa that establish slowly, expand after an initial lag, or matter mainly through later descendant founding receive little or no credit, so optimization is biased toward immediate occupancy bursts instead of delayed-payoff diversification. Intervention: add lifetime and delayed-payoff activity measures that attribute post-origin occupancy, abundance, and downstream founding across subsequent windows rather than truncating credit at the birth window.
- [expand] [Resource Landscape Collinearity] `regenerateResources()` grows both `resources` and `resources2` from the same `effectiveBiomeFertilityAt()` scalar and the same seasonal regeneration multiplier, while `buildBiomeFertility()` creates only one underlying fertility field. That means the dual-resource world still offers one spatial opportunity landscape expressed twice: cells rich for the primary substrate are structurally also rich for the secondary substrate up to global scale differences, so the system cannot produce orthogonal resource hotspots, cross-gradients, or place-based partitioning where different specialists succeed in different regions because each substrate peaks in different places. Intervention: give each resource layer its own fertility / regeneration field or a shared latent basis with independent per-layer weights and phases, and expose cross-layer correlation as a first-class control.
- [expand] [Composition-Agnostic Metabolism] `spendAgentEnergy()` debits both `energyPrimary` and `energySecondary` proportionally to current mix; movement cost, metabolism, encounter transfers, and disturbance impacts key off total energy only. Reproduction now gates on minimum pool fractions, but all other physiology treats specialists with imbalanced pools identically to generalists with matched total energy. Intervention: make metabolism, movement, or encounter outcomes depend on pool-specific requirements or genome-conditioned target ratios.
- [expand] [Resource Transport Ceiling] `resources` and `resources2` only change through local regeneration, local harvest, disturbance scaling, and corpse-derived spillover to the four cardinal neighbors. There is no diffusion, advection, or patch-to-patch transport operator, so the system cannot express moving nutrient fronts, subsidy corridors, source-sink rescue via imported resources, or persistent depletion halos replenished by lateral flow no matter how the Planner retunes current parameters. Intervention: add per-layer transport dynamics such as diffusion, directional flow, or graph-edge exchange as explicit mechanism slots executed each tick, and expose transport coefficients independently from harvest and regeneration.
- [expand] [Non-Heritable Demography] Core life-history controls remain global `SimulationConfig` constants: `reproduceThreshold`, `reproduceProbability`, `offspringEnergyFraction`, and `maxAge` do not live in `Genome` or any mutable per-agent state. Within that architecture, lineages cannot evolve different reproductive schedules, starvation tolerance, parental investment fractions, or senescence timing except indirectly through energy acquisition, so fast-versus-slow demographic strategies are structurally unavailable regardless of planner optimization. Intervention: promote key demographic controls into heritable loci or stage-specific policy parameters with mutation and inheritance, then let survival and reproduction consult those evolved values instead of only run-wide constants.
- [expand] [Biota-Independent Productivity] `regenerateResources()` rebuilds both resource layers from `effectiveBiomeFertilityAt()` and seasonal multipliers alone; consumers can reduce standing stock, but they never alter the future regeneration law except through transient corpse spillover. That assumption rules out overexploitation-driven productivity collapse, facilitation-driven productivity gains, alternative productivity states, and producer-consumer feedback loops because the ecosystem's productive capacity is exogenous rather than ecologically responsive. Intervention: replace fixed per-cell regeneration with stateful substrate or producer dynamics whose recovery rates depend on local biotic pressure, deposits, disturbance history, or habitat state, and make those state variables part of the simulation and study surface.
- [expand] [Trait Decoder Bottleneck] `GenomeV2` can store arbitrary trait keys, but the live simulator still routes ecology through hard-coded reads of `metabolism`, `harvest`, `aggression`, `harvestEfficiency2`, and taxon-level habitat / trophic / defense caches. Under this architecture any newly added locus is selectively inert baggage until a human wires it into a specific operator, so schema expansion alone cannot yield new phenotypes or interactions. Intervention: replace per-operator hard-coded trait access with a trait-expression registry or phenotype decoder that maps declared loci into metabolism, harvest, encounter, movement, settlement, and taxonomic decisions. Within that geometry, added complexity is limited to "more numbers in a map," while speciation and cladogenesis remain sensitive to arbitrary scaling choices rather than semantic novelty.
