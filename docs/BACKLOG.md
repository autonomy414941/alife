Intervention: make graded harvest preserve and transform the inherited base preference rather than overwrite it with a threshold-only curve, then revalidate the March 26 neutrality claim.
- [expand] [Trait Decoder Bottleneck] `GenomeV2` can store arbitrary trait keys, but the live simulator still routes ecology through hard-coded reads of a small fixed set of loci and taxon caches. - [refactor] [Policy Mutation Architecture Drift] Policy mutation, clamping, removability, and distance categorization are still spread across hard-coded trait lists and switch-like branches. ## Critic — 2026-03-27

- [expand] [Phenotype Realization Gap] `GenomeV2` stores arbitrary trait keys, `genome-v2.ts` centralizes trait metadata (roles, clamps, distance categories, activation modes), and `StepSummary` now includes `genomeV2Metrics` for genome-wide observability. However, the core simulation operators (movement, harvest, encounters, reproduction) still read only a hard-coded subset of legacy traits (`metabolism`, `harvest`, `aggression`) plus a few opt-in ecological loci. The metadata is consulted for mutation and distance but not for ecology. **Structural ceiling**: A new `GenomeV2` trait cannot affect fitness, settlement, or foraging unless a human explicitly threads it through one or more hard-coded simulation paths. Intervention: replace direct trait reads in ecology operators with a phenotype decoder or trait-to-effect registry that maps all defined traits to their ecological roles automatically.

- [expand] [Policy-Ecology Segregation] Policy traits (thresholds, steepnesses, preferences) are now unified into `genomeV2.traits`, mutated via `policyMutationProbability`, classified by distance category, and observable in `policyObservability` summaries. But they remain architecturally distinct: they gate decisions (movement, reproduction, harvest allocation) without directly changing ecological capabilities (foraging efficiency, metabolic cost, defense). **Structural ceiling**: A policy locus can alter when an agent acts but not what resources it can extract or how much energy it burns. This prevents the evolution of integrated strategies where decision thresholds and ecological efficiency co-adapt. Intervention: allow policy parameters to modulate core ecological rates (e.g., threshold-dependent harvest efficiency, energy-gated metabolic cost scaling) or expose policy state as a readable ecological trait to other agents.

- [validate] [Symmetric Environment Nullifies Policy Differentiation] The world presents two resource layers (`resource`, `resource2`) on the same `fertility` map with the same seasonal forcing (`seasonalRegenAmplitude`, `seasonalFertilityContrastAmplitude`). Policy loci like `harvest_secondary_preference` and `spending_secondary_preference` can tune substrate allocation, but both pools regenerate identically, so substrate choice is ecologically neutral under the default configuration. **Structural ceiling**: Even correctly expressed, heritable harvest and spending policies cannot drive niche differentiation when both resources offer symmetric payoffs. Intervention: decouple resource regeneration (independent spatial patterns, temporal cycles, or disturbance responses per layer) so substrate specialization becomes an ecological tradeoff rather than a bookkeeping preference.

- [expand] [Context-Blind Trait Expression] All traits are expressed as scalar genotype values with fixed clamps and defaults. The phenotype is the genotype, independent of local habitat, energy state, age, seasonal phase, or recent history. **Structural ceiling**: The system cannot evolve conditional strategies (habitat-specific harvest efficiency, age-dependent aggression, energy-gated metabolic efficiency) because trait expression has no environmental input. Intervention: add a phenotype construction layer that maps `(genomeV2, environmentContext) → expressedTraits` so heritable loci can be modulated by abiotic or biotic context at decision time.

- [validate] [Unbounded Trait Accumulation] `GenomeV2` allows arbitrary trait accumulation up to `maxTraits=20`. Traits marked `activationMode: 'presence'` count toward distance and mutation regardless of their value, and traits with unbounded clamps (e.g., `policyThreshold` with `clamp: {min: 0}`) can drift to arbitrarily large values. **Structural ceiling**: Taxonomic identity can be inflated by accumulating inert or ecologically redundant traits, and distance-driven speciation can be triggered by threshold drift even when thresholds exceed ecologically relevant ranges. Intervention: add trait-activation energy costs, prune traits below activation thresholds during reproduction, or impose range-aware distance normalization so extreme threshold values beyond the decision domain are downweighted.

- [feat] [No Emergent Trait Construction] The trait catalog is fixed at compile time via `GENOME_V2_TRAIT_DEFINITIONS`. Adding a new ecological dimension (e.g., temperature tolerance, toxin resistance, substrate transformation) requires manual addition to the central registry and wiring through simulator operators. **Structural ceiling**: The system cannot discover new phenotypic dimensions beyond those a human pre-specified. Intervention: replace the fixed trait catalog with a trait-module generator that composes new loci from parameterized templates (e.g., `thresholdGate(targetResource, responseMode)`, `efficiencyModulator(substratePair, tradeoffCurve)`) or allow emergent trait construction through genome rearrangement operators.
