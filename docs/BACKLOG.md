- [strategize] [Single-Mechanism Tuning Ceiling] The current research surface sweeps numeric and boolean fields in `SimulationConfig` (founder grace ticks, crowding penalties, clade coupling scalars), while the architecture remains locked to one resource pool, three genome axes, greedy one-step movement, dominant-only encounters, and clonal inheritance. - [expand] [Energy Metabolism Fungibility] `Agent` has only one energy scalar (`agent.energy`), and `metabolism`, `harvest`, `predation`, `reproduction`, and `decomposition` all debit or credit this single currency. Resource acquisition is mediated by two substrate pools (`resources` and `resources2`) with genome axes for differential harvest efficiency (`genome.harvest`, `genome.harvestEfficiency2`), but energy internally is completely fungible—energy gained from resource1 is indistinguishable from energy gained from resource2 once inside the agent, and all metabolic, reproductive, and predation operations draw on the same unified pool. This topology structurally caps the expressiveness of resource-partitioning and multi-nutrient tradeoffs: under fungible internal energy, an agent that harvests 50% resource1 and 50% resource2 is metabolically identical to an agent that harvests 100% resource1, as long as total energy gained is equal. Specialists and generalists converge to the same internal state if their total energy income matches, erasing the distinction between resource-exploitation strategies once harvest completes. No amount of tuning harvest-efficiency genome parameters can produce metabolic constraints, cofactor limitations, or toxicity / balance effects where excess of one resource cannot substitute for deficiency in another. Intervention: replace the single `agent.energy` scalar with a structured internal state carrying separate resource-derived pools or add a multi-nutrient bookkeeping layer where reproduction, metabolism, and survival require specific resource ratios rather than arbitrary energy sums. Alternatively, introduce conversion inefficiencies or metabolic penalties when internal energy composition deviates from an agent's genome-defined optimum, so that harvest-efficiency diversity translates into persistent internal differentiation instead of collapsing to one fungible sum. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Reproduction Energy Transfer Simplicity] `reproduceInSimulation()` creates exactly one offspring per successful reproduction event, always transfers exactly `offspringEnergyFraction * parentEnergy` into that single child, and decrements the parent's unified `energy` scalar by the same amount. This architecture cannot express variable litter sizes, differential offspring provisioning, or energy-allocation strategies where parents split energy unevenly among multiple offspring, or where offspring cost depends on environmental conditions or parent state beyond a fixed fraction. Combined with fungible internal energy, reproduction becomes a single-dimensional scalar transfer with no opportunity for offspring-quality versus offspring-quantity tradeoffs, no clutch-composition strategies, and no structured allocation of different internal resource pools to different offspring roles (e.g., dispersers versus competitors). Within this ceiling, life-history diversity is limited to threshold-and-probability gates on when to reproduce, not how to allocate reproductive investment. Intervention: replace single-child fixed-fraction reproduction with a variable-offspring allocation operator that can emit zero to many offspring per event, each with explicit energy provisioning decided by heritable parameters and local conditions, and allow reproduction cost to draw differentially from structured internal resource pools if those are introduced. Dimension: Expressiveness.
- [expand] [Encounter Resolution Invariance Under Fungible Energy] The March 17 comparative study at canonical 4000-step horizon confirmed that pairwise versus dominant encounter operators produce identical coexistence outcomes (zero delta at all tested thresholds, both operators converge to single active clade, mean active-clade delta versus matched null = 0 for both). The nullity diagnostics reveal structural inevitability: mean energy transfer per encounter is nearly identical (dominant 1.097, pairwise 1.102, delta 0.005), aggression hierarchies stabilize to the same rank order (Kendall tau ~0.65), and spatial clustering of aggressive phenotypes remains unchanged. Under fungible internal energy, encounter topology (pairwise versus dominant resolution order) only changes the accounting sequence, not who wins or loses—aggressive agents always extract energy from less-aggressive agents at the same per-encounter rate regardless of resolution order, and extracted energy is indistinguishable from harvested energy once inside the winner. The dual-resource substrate added in commit `0a069b9` provides harvest-efficiency differentiation at intake, but once energy enters the unified internal pool, predation and encounter outcomes depend only on total energy and aggression, erasing any upstream resource-specialization signal. Within this topology, no encounter operator variant can produce interaction-driven coexistence if all agents compete through the same fungible energy currency and metabolic cost structure. Intervention: make encounter energy transfers conditional on internal resource composition (e.g., predators preferentially extract certain resource-derived pools, or transfers incur conversion costs if predator and prey have mismatched internal compositions), or introduce non-energy interaction outcomes such as spatial displacement, trait modification, or ephemeral status effects that persist beyond a single-tick energy accounting step. Dimension: Expressiveness.
